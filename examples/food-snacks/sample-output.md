# Heritage Bites — Creative Direction Pack
### Product Launch Poster | Instagram 4:5 | Podi Varieties

---

## 1. BRIEF UNDERSTANDING

**Client:** Heritage Bites
**Goal:** Announce the launch of new homemade podi varieties
**Platform:** Instagram (4:5 ratio — 1080 × 1350 px)
**Core message:** "Homemade podi varieties now available"
**Brand personality:** Authentic, warm, homemade, traditional

The poster needs to feel like something your grandmother would proudly display — not a generic food brand ad. It should evoke the smell of freshly ground spices, the warmth of a traditional kitchen, and the trust that comes from handmade quality.

---

## 2. ASSUMPTIONS

> *(Labeled clearly — adjust if any of these don't match your actual product)*

- **A1:** The podi varieties include classics like Idli Podi, Dosa Podi, Curry Leaf Podi, Flaxseed Podi, and/or Coconut Podi. *(Update variety names as needed.)*
- **A2:** The product is packaged in glass jars or kraft paper pouches with handwritten-style labels.
- **A3:** The target audience is South Indian households, food lovers aged 25–50, and nostalgic NRIs who miss home-style flavors.
- **A4:** No specific discount or offer — this is a pure awareness/launch post.
- **A5:** Brand has an earthy, artisanal aesthetic — not clinical or ultra-modern.

---

## 3. CREATIVE STRATEGY

**Emotional direction:** Nostalgia meets pride. The poster should make the viewer feel like they're discovering a family secret — a grandmother's recipe made available for the first time.

**Single core idea:**
> *"The flavors your kitchen has been missing."*

**Why this works:**
- Podi is deeply tied to memory — Sunday morning idlis, school-day dosai, home-cooked meals
- "Homemade" is the brand's biggest differentiator in a market full of mass-produced versions
- The launch moment is best framed as a discovery, not a sale

**Tone:** Warm. Proud. Unhurried. Like a handwritten recipe card passed down through generations.

---

## 4. VISUAL DIRECTION

**Overall aesthetic:** Artisan food photography meets traditional South Indian kitchen warmth.

**Scene concept:**
A centered product arrangement — 3 to 5 podi jars/pouches grouped naturally on a rustic wooden board or dark stone slab. The background is a deep warm terracotta or aged brass tone. Subtle traditional elements appear softly out of focus in the background — a brass uruli, dried red chilies on a string, curry leaf sprigs, or a stone mortar and pestle partially visible at an edge.

**Lighting direction:** Soft, warm, directional overhead light (like late afternoon kitchen light). Slight rim light on the jars to make them feel premium. No harsh shadows.

**Depth of field:** Shallow — foreground and background elements are gently blurred, keeping the products crisp and centered.

**Mood board cues:** Think: handcrafted, golden warmth, stone textures, dried spices, glass and kraft, candlelight glow — not clinical white studio lighting.

## 5. COLOR PALETTE

| Role | Color | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Background | Deep Terracotta | `#8B3A2A` | Main background wash |
| Warm Accent | Aged Saffron | `#D4882E` | Headline text, dividers |
| Neutral Base | Parchment Cream | `#F5EDDA` | Body text, tag lines |
| Dark Anchor | Burnt Umber | `#3D1C0E` | Borders, shadow overlays |
| Highlight | Golden Brass | `#C9960C` | Logo, small decorative elements |

**Color rationale:** This palette mirrors the natural tones of dried spices — chili red, turmeric gold, tamarind brown. It signals "real ingredients" without a word. Avoid pastel or cool tones — they will kill the warmth.

---

## 6. TYPOGRAPHY DIRECTION

**Primary Headline (Variety names / Main CTA):**
- Style: Serif with slight calligraphic personality
- Suggested fonts: *Cormorant Garamond Bold*, *Playfair Display*, or *EB Garamond*
- Feel: Handcrafted elegance — not rigid or corporate

**Secondary Text (Brand name / Tagline):**
- Style: Clean, slightly condensed serif or an earthy script
- Suggested fonts: *Libre Baskerville*, *Lora*, or *Pacifico* (for script elements only)
- Feel: Approachable, readable, warm

**Accent / Label text (Ingredients hint, "Homemade" badge):**
- Style: All-caps, tight letter spacing
- Suggested font: *Raleway* or *Josefin Sans* (uppercase, tracked wide)
- Feel: Artisan product label

**Hierarchy rule:**
1. Brand name (top, smaller — establishes authority)
2. Hero headline / product announcement (center-top or overlaid on product)
3. Variety names (near/on product, medium weight)
4. Tagline or secondary message (bottom, smallest)

**Do NOT mix more than 3 typefaces. 2 is ideal.**

---

## 7. LAYOUT DIRECTION

**Canvas:** 1080 × 1350 px (Instagram 4:5)

**Grid structure:**

```
┌─────────────────────────────┐
│   [Brand Name / Logo]        │  ← Top 12% — centered or left-aligned
│                              │
│  [Subtle bg texture/          │
│   traditional elements]      │  ← Full bleed warm background
│                              │
│   ┌──────────────────────┐   │
│   │   PRODUCT HERO       │   │  ← Center 55% — main product card/group
│   │   (Jars/Pouches)     │   │
│   └──────────────────────┘   │
│                              │
│  [Headline Text]             │  ← 70–80% mark — main copy
│  [Variety names / badges]    │
│                              │
│  [Tagline + CTA]             │  ← Bottom 10% — call to action
└─────────────────────────────┘
```

**Visual weight flow:** Eyes enter at the product → read the headline → scan variety names → land on the CTA.

**White space:** Generous. Don't crowd the canvas. Let the product breathe. Padding of at least 60–80 px on all edges.

**Background texture:** A very subtle grain/noise overlay (5–10% opacity) over the background adds a handcrafted, printed feel. Avoid flat digital color.

## 9. NEGATIVE PROMPT / AVOID LIST

**Visual avoids:**
- ❌ White or light backgrounds — kills warmth entirely
- ❌ Cold blue/purple lighting or tones
- ❌ Stock photo generic "spice bowls" with no brand identity
- ❌ Busy, crowded layout — less is more here
- ❌ Neon or high-saturation modern color accents
- ❌ Sans-serif only typography — feels too tech/corporate
- ❌ Watermarks, realistic UI mockups, or phone screens in the poster
- ❌ More than 4–5 typefaces
- ❌ Drop shadows that look like 2010 PowerPoint

**Copy avoids:**
- ❌ Generic phrases: "Now Available!", "Try Our Products!", "Taste the Difference"
- ❌ Overloading the poster with ingredient lists or long text blocks
- ❌ Screaming all-caps for the entire headline

**Concept avoids:**
- ❌ Copying the reference poster's exact layout, framing, or color scheme
- ❌ Making it look like a restaurant menu — this is a product launch, not a food service ad
- ❌ Losing the "homemade" softness by over-polishing into a luxury brand aesthetic

- [ ] Set brand name at top — small, spaced, elegant
- [ ] Place headline copy below product: "Homemade Podi Varieties · Now Available"
- [ ] Add variety names — either as small chips/badges near jars or as a row below headline
- [ ] Add bottom tagline or soft CTA ("Order via DM / Link in Bio")
- [ ] Apply grain overlay on final composite
- [ ] Export at 1080 × 1350 px, sRGB, JPEG/PNG at 90%+ quality

**Before posting:**
- [ ] View at phone screen size — does the headline read in 2 seconds?
- [ ] Check contrast: text must be legible against background
- [ ] Verify no important content falls in Instagram's bottom overlay zone (bottom 14%)

## 12. OPTIONAL VARIATIONS

**Variation A — Light version:**
Swap the deep terracotta for a parchment cream `#F5EDDA` background. Use dark burnt umber `#3D1C0E` for all type. Works well for Stories or a carousel second slide. Feels more editorial, slightly less dramatic.

**Variation B — Single variety spotlight:**
One jar, centered, full frame. Large variety name as headline. Micro-copy: "Part of our new homemade collection." Works as a series — one post per podi variety. Great for phased launch content.

**Variation C — Flat lay grid:**
5 pouches/jars arranged in a flat lay grid from above. Equal spacing, overhead light. Minimal text — just brand name and "5 Varieties. All Homemade." Clean, modern-artisan feel. Good for the feed grid aesthetic.
